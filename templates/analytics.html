Primary Finding

The Household
Multiplier Effect

Account sharing and family profiles serve as a powerful retention mechanism. When multiple household members rely on a single subscription, the perceived cost-per-user decreases dramatically — making the account highly resilient to cancellation.

Retained Users (Label 0)

Maintain an average of 3.1 to 3.5 profiles per account

Churned Users (Label 1)

Drop below an average of 3 profiles (typically 2.6 to 2.9)

Critical Threshold

The Engagement
Drop-Off Cliff

There is a strict engagement threshold below which users are at an extreme statistical risk of cancellation. The data reveals a dramatic cliff — not a gradual decline — in weekly consumption hours between retained and churned subscribers.

Retained Users (Label 0)

Consume an average of 16 to 20 weekly watch hours

Churned Users (Label 1)

Crater to a critical danger zone of 5.5 to 6.4 weekly hours
